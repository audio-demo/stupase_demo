StuPASE: Towards Low-Hallucination, Studio-Quality Generative Speech Enhancement

Anonymous Submission

Abstract

Hallucination is a critical issue in generative speech enhancement (SE). A representative approach, PASE, demonstrates strong robustness against hallucination under highly challenging acoustic conditions, yet its perceptual quality remains limited. In this paper, we propose StuPASE, building upon PASE to achieve studio-level quality while retaining its low-hallucination property. We make two key technical contributions. First, we show that PASE's degraded performance under severe reverberation largely stems from non-anechoic training targets: by removing early reflections in the training targets, simple fine-tuning of PASE yields substantial improvements in dereverberation. Second, to address performance limitations under strong additive noise, we replace the simple GAN-based vocoder in PASE with a flow-matching module, enabling studio-quality generation even under highly noisy conditions. Experimental results demonstrate that StuPASE consistently improves perceptual quality while preserving the low-hallucination property of PASE, outperforming existing state-of-the-art SE methods.
